      Journalism Disrupted, Journalism Reborn: The Future of Media in a Digital World

      Register Now

    What is the future of journalism?

    It’s no longer a matter of debate; journalism is being disrupted. While its future is unknown, what we can be certain of is that social media is erecting new forms of storytelling, bringing remarkable stories to life. Nevertheless, does this mean there could be an end to quality journalism as we know it? Or, will the disruption of social media mean greater access to news and information like never before.

    In this free panel, we’ll hear from news organizations on how they are managing the shift to social platforms, and from the platforms themselves on how they’re upholding their responsibility to an informed citizenry.

    Moderator

    Vivian Schiller

    Executive Editor-in-Residence, Weber Shandwick

    Vivian is a longtime executive at the intersection of journalism, media and technology. Most recently, Schiller served as the Global Chair of News at Twitter. She has held several executive roles including President & CEO of NPR, Chief Digital Officer for NBC News, General Manager of NYTimes.com, as well as positions at Discovery and CNN.

    Topics Covered

    The State of Journalism

    • Journalism is being disrupted and it’s future is unclear. However, social media and quality journalism CAN co-exist.

    Social Media

    • Social media is fueling the way news is viewed like never before. Instant news does not equate to sub-par news.

    What's winning

    • Social platforms and traditional media can co-exist. How they’re maintaining a symbiotic relationship.

    Panelists

    Connie Guglielmo

    Editor in Chief, CNET News

    Connie is responsible for overseeing the global news team as they report on breaking news, and deliver analysis, profiles, Q&As and long-form feature stories about the people, companies and technology changing the world. Before joining CNET in 2014, she was as a tech business reporter  at Forbes and Bloomberg, covering Apple (among others). Before that, she worked in and around Silicon Valley for publications including MacWeek, Wired, Upside and Interactive Week.

    MC Wellons

    CNBC's San Francisco Bureau Chief

    Mary Catherine Wellons is CNBC’s San Francisco bureau chief, responsible for spearheading CNBC’s broadcast and digital coverage of the technology industry at the network’s new bureau. Prior to moving to San Francisco, Wellons was CNBC’s first-ever director of social media, where she built a team responsible for creating content across Twitter, Facebook, and emerging media platforms. Wellons also was recently named to LinkedIn’s Next Wave List of top professionals under 35

    Joaquin Alvarado

    CEO, Center for Investigative Reporting

    Joaquin Alvarado is the CEO for The Center for Investigative Reporting. Before joining CIR, Joaquin served as senior vice president for digital innovation at American Public Media and founding senior vice president for diversity and innovation at the Corporation for Public Broadcasting. He is the founder of CoCo Studios, which promotes media collaboration and game development for fiber and mobile networks, and the founding director of the Institute for Next Generation Internet.

    Ann Grimes

    Professor at Stanford and former Wall Street Journal SF Bureau Chief

    Ann Grimes currently serves as Lorry I. Lokey Professor of the Practice at Stanford University, teaching classes in technology reporting, digital media and entrepreneurship. Formerly, she was staff writer and editor for The Wall Street Journal where she covered technology and business. As Deputy Bureau Chief in San Francisco, she oversaw the newspaper’s coverage of Silicon Valley during the 1990′s dot-com boom and bust. While at Dow Jones & Co., she worked on developing new media strategy.

    Caroline Fairchild

    New Economy Editor at LinkedIn

    Caroline Fairchild joined LinkedIn this year as the New Economy Editor to oversee coverage of the venture capital industry and the world of startups. In her role, she reports and writes timely stories , works with venture capitalists and entrepreneurs who write on LinkedIn and curates the entrepreneurship and venture capital news channels on LinkedIn Pulse. Her goal is to make LinkedIn the premium destination for business news. Before starting at LinkedIn, Fairchild founded and wrote The Broadsheet, Fortune's fast-growing daily newsletter about women leaders.
